Alternatives to Payday Loans

Better Options When You Need Cash Fast

← Back to paydayloandebt.org

Credit Union Payday Alternative Loans

PALs from federal credit unions offer $200-$2,000 at up to 28% APR (vs. 300%+ for payday loans). Joining a credit union costs $5-25. You must be a member for 1 month to qualify for PAL I. These are real loans with real underwriting, not predatory products. If you do not have a credit union, find one at mycreditunion.gov.

Cash Advance Apps

Apps like Earnin, Dave, and Brigit offer small advances ($50-$500) on your paycheck with no interest. Some request optional tips. These are not loans -- they advance money you have already earned. They are dramatically cheaper than payday loans. However, they do not solve underlying cash flow problems and can create their own cycle of dependency.

Employer Cash Advances

Many employers offer paycheck advances at no cost. Ask your HR department. Some employers partner with earned wage access (EWA) providers like DailyPay, PayActiv, or Even that let you access earned wages before payday. Walmart, Target, and many other large employers offer these services.

Community and Government Resources

Before borrowing at all, check: 211 (local assistance), SNAP/food stamps, LIHEAP (utility help), food banks, community action agencies, church/synagogue emergency funds, and rental assistance programs. Many communities have emergency assistance that provides grants (not loans) for immediate needs. Apply to everything -- the worst that happens is they say no.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are cash advance apps safe?

Generally yes, though read the terms carefully. Some request large tips that approach loan-level costs. Stick to apps that clearly disclose all costs and do not pressure tipping. Never use an app that requires access to your bank credentials.

Can I get a personal loan with bad credit?

Credit unions are the best option for personal loans with bad credit. Online lenders like LendingClub and Upstart also work with lower credit scores, though rates will be higher. Avoid any lender charging more than 36% APR.

What if I need money today and cannot wait?

Call 211 for immediate local assistance. Visit a food bank to free up grocery money. Ask family or friends for a short-term loan. Sell items on Facebook Marketplace or OfferUp. Donate plasma ($50-75 per visit). These options are all better than a payday loan.
